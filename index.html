Publications

F2former: When Fractional Fourier Meets Deep Wiener Deconvolution and Selective Frequency Transformer for Image Deblurring

NEW Subhajit Paul, Sahil Kumawat, Ashutosh Gupta, Deepak Mishra
Preprint, 2024

We introduce a novel image deblurring based on the Fractional Fourier Transform (FRFT), a spatial-frequency representation, making it ideal for processing non-stationary signals like images. Here, we present a Fractional Fourier Transformer (F2former), by combining the fractional Fourier based fwature Wiener deconvolution and a multi-branch encoder-decoder transformer based on a new fractional frequency aware transformer block. We design it using a fractional frequency aware self-attention (F2SA) to estimate element-wise product attention based on important frequency components and a novel feed-forward network based on frequency division multiplexing to refine high and low frequency features separately for efficient latent clear image restoration. Experimental results for the cases of both motion and defocus deblurring show that the performance of our proposed method is superior to other SOTA approaches.

A Sinkhorn Regularized Adversarial Network for Image Guided DEM Super-resolution using Frequency Selective Hybrid Graph Transformer

Subhajit Paul, Ashutosh Gupta
ICPR, 2024 (First round acceptance rate: 31%)

We propose a novel hybrid transformer model for generating high-resolution DEMs using multi-spectral satellite imagery as guide. The model combines Densely connected Multi-Residual Blocks (DMRB) with multi-headed Frequency Selective Graph Attention (M-FSGA), using discriminator spatial maps as attention to the guide. We introduce an adversarial objective based on Sinkhorn distance to improve performance of GANs, addressing vanishing gradients and numerical convergence. Experiments across four DEM datasets demonstrate that our model outperforms baselines with sharper details and minimal errors.

C-LiSA: A Hierarchical Hybrid Transformer Model using Orthogonal Cross Attention for Satellite Image Cloud Segmentation

Subhajit Paul, Ashutosh Gupta, S. Manthira Moorthi, Debajyoti Dhar
IEEE Transaction on Geoscience and Remote Sensing (TGRS) (Impact Factor: 8.2)

We introduce a hybrid transformer architecture for precise cloud mask generation. The model employs dual orthogonal self-attention (DOSA) and hierarchical cross-channel attention (HC2A) to enhance cloud-specific feature detection. To ensure stability, we theoretically validate these mechanisms with Lipschitz stability analysis with empirical validation against FGSM and PGD-20 attack. We also employ adversarial training with Lovász-Softmax loss. Experiments on satellite datasets, including Landsat-8 and Sentinel-2, show that C-LiSA outperforms state-of-the-art methods, with improved generalization and superior results.

A framework for Realistic Paired Dataset Generation for Deep Learning based Restoration of Satellite Images

Ashutosh Gupta, Subhajit Paul, Arghya Bhattacharya, Prachi Jain

We propose a framework using a GAN and conventional degradation estimation techniques to generate paired datasets for satellite image restoration. The surrogate datasets are generated from images with desirable characteristics, enabling deep learning models to achieve impressive restoration results without requiring actual clean data. Experiments on 30 cm Worldview-3 and Cartosat-3 images show that this approach is both simple and effective for satellite image restoration.

High-resolution Multi-spectral Image Guided DEM Super-resolution using Sinkhorn Regularized Adversarial Network

Subhajit Paul, Ashutosh Gupta
ICLR Machine Learning 4 Remote Sensing (ML4RS) workshop, 2024 (shorter version)

We introduce an adversarial model for generating high-resolution DEMs using HR multi-spectral satellite imagery as a prior, with discriminator activations serving as spatial attention. The model incorporates a Dense Multi-Residual Block (DMRB) for efficient gradient flow and uses Sinkhorn distance with GAN to improve adversarial stability. Experiments on SRTM and Cartosat-1 DEM datasets demonstrate superior performance over state-of-the-art methods, both qualitatively and quantitatively. We also visualize high-resolution DEMs across diverse terrains to validate our model’s effectiveness.

Hiding Images within Audio using Deep Generative Model

Subhajit Paul, Deepak Mishra
Multimedia Tools and Applications, (Impact Factor: 3.6)

We introduce a method to hide images within audio by transforming the audio steganography problem into image steganography using mel-spectrograms as the cover medium. Using deep generative models, our approach enhances the perceptual quality of the reconstructed audio and images, while effectively evading detection by steganalysis tools.

Reconstruction of Missing Multiband Images for High Resolution Multispectral Sensors Using Wasserstein GAN

Md Aminur Hossain, Ashutosh Gupta, Subhajit Paul, Sanjay K Singh, S. Devakanth Naidu, Debajyoti Dhar
IEEE InGARSS, 2023

We propose a method to reconstruct lost multiband regions in MX images using a WGAN with expert regularization. Our approach synthesizes RGB bands from co-registered NIR and PAN images. Experiments on Cartosat-MX images show that the reconstructed images are spatially and spectrally accurate.

DEM Generation from Mono-Multispectral Images via Uncalibrated Epipolar Rectification and 3D Registration

Ashutosh Gupta, Hemant K Lalwawni, Subhajit Paul, Debajyoti Dhar
IEEE RASSE, 2023

We propose a novel DEM generation pipeline that leverages narrow-baseline information from a single multispectral image to produce sub-pixel accurate disparity maps, addressing spectral variations. The final DEM is generated using reference Ground Control Points (GCPs) and iterative registration. Applied to Resourcesat-2 LISS-IV images with a low BIH ratio of 0.018, our method computes sub-pixel disparity at 1/50th of a pixel. Comparison with Cartosat-1 DEMs shows strong elevation accuracy, even in hilly regions, enabling DEM generation from multispectral images with low B/H ratios.
