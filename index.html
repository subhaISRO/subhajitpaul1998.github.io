About

I am a working as a Scientist/Engineer-SC at the Space Applications Centre (SAC), Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). My work focuses on developing advanced image processing and computer vision solutions to enhance radiometric quality of high-resolution satellite imagery. In addition, I am engaged in designing operational deep learning solutions for image inverse problems and segmentation domain. My research interests also include theoretically analysis of model stability, robustness and convergence properties. I have a deep interest in investigating traditional image processing and optimization techniques to develop reliable cutting edge deep learning solutions for image inverse problems.

I received my B.Tech from Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology (IIST). I was fornuate to get the opprtunity to work at Virtual Reality (VR) lab under the supervision of Prof. Deepak Mishra during my undergrad research.

Publications

F2former: When Fractional Fourier Meets Deep Wiener Deconvolution and Selective Frequency Transformer for Image Deblurring

NEW Subhajit Paul, Sahil Kumawat, Ashutosh Gupta, Deepak Mishra

We introduce a novel image deblurring based on the Fractional Fourier Transform (FRFT), a spatial-frequency representation, making it ideal for processing non-stationary signals like images. Here, we present a Fractional Fourier Transformer (F2former), by combining the fractional Fourier based fwature Wiener deconvolution and a multi-branch encoder-decoder transformer based on a new fractional frequency aware transformer block. We design it using a fractional frequency aware self-attention (F2SA) to estimate element-wise product attention based on important frequency components and a novel feed-forward network based on frequency division multiplexing to refine high and low frequency features separately for efficient latent clear image restoration. Experimental results for the cases of both motion and defocus deblurring show that the performance of our proposed method is superior to other SOTA approaches.

Beyond First-order Tweedie: Solving Inverse Problems using Latent Diffusion

NEW Litu Rout, Yujia Chen, Abhishek Kumar, Constantine Caramanis, Sanjay Shakkottai

We present an efficient second-order approximation using Tweedie’s formula to mitigate the bias incurred in widely used first-order samplers. With this method, we devise a surrogate loss function for every diffusion step to address inverse problems.
